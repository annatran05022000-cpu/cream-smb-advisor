## Step 1: Know Your Market

### The Four Types of Small Business Owner Email List

Not all small business owner email list is created equal. Understanding the different categories helps you allocate resources effectively:

**1. Inbound Lead Generation**
SEO and content marketing bring warm prospects to you. Best long-term ROI, but takes 6-12 months to build meaningful volume.

**2. Outbound Prospecting**
Direct outreach to targeted contacts. You control the volume, the timing, and the ICP — at the cost of higher per-lead investment.

**3. Paid Acquisition**
Ads capture leads through forms and landing pages — fast to scale, measurable, and more expensive than direct outreach.

**4. Partner and Referral**
Partner-sourced and referred leads close faster and at higher rates. The limitation is that volume depends on others, not your own actions.

For most SMB businesses, a combination of outbound prospecting and inbound content produces the best risk-adjusted results.

## Step 3: Write for Response

### Cold Email Structure That Gets Responses

Cold emails with personalized subject lines get 50% higher open rates, per [Yes Lifecycle Marketing](https://yesmarketing.com/resources/).

The characteristics that determine whether a cold email gets read, considered, or deleted:

**Subject line principles:**
- Under 7 words performs best on mobile
- Avoid spam trigger words (free, guaranteed, urgent)
- Reference something specific to the prospect when possible
- Questions often outperform statements

**Body copy principles:**
- First sentence should not mention your company
- Establish relevance before making any ask
- One value proposition, not three
- One call to action, not options

**Example high-converting sequence for SMB:**

*Email 1 (Day 1):*
Subject: One question about [specific challenge] at [their company]

"[Name], quick question — saw [company signal] and wanted to reach out. Teams in your situation usually run into [specific pain]. Is that something you're actively solving?"

*Email 2 (Day 5):*
Subject: Re: [original subject]

"Following up once more. If [challenge] comes up, happy to share a specific example. If not the right time, totally fine too — just let me know either way."

*Email 3 (Day 12):*
Subject: Last note on this

"Last message from me. If [pain point] ever becomes a priority, happy to pick up the conversation — [email]. Good luck with [specific thing they have going on]."

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is a good cold email open rate for B2B?**

A 25-35% open rate is considered good for B2B cold email. Top performers achieve 40-50%. If you're below 20%, the problem is usually your subject line or sender reputation. **How many follow-up emails should I send?**

Research consistently shows 5-7 follow-ups is optimal. According to [The Brevet Group](https://thebrevetgroup.com/50-pro-sales-statistics/), 80% of sales require at least 5 follow-up contacts. Most teams give up after 1-2, which means they're walking away from the majority of their potential responses.

**What are the best days and times to send cold emails?**

Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday mornings (7-9am recipient's local time) consistently produce the highest open and reply rates. Avoid Monday mornings (inbox catch-up) and Friday afternoons (end-of-week wind-down). For US-based prospects, schedule by their time zone.
